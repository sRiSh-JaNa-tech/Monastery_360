Lingdum Monastery

About Lingdum (Ranka) Monastery

Lingdum Monastery, also popularly known as Ranka Monastery, is a magnificent Buddhist complex located in East Sikkim, near Gangtok. Though relatively new, it has quickly become a major attraction for its stunning architecture, vast courtyards, and deeply serene environment. It follows the Zurmang Kagyu lineage of Tibetan Buddhism and serves as an important training center for young lamas.

The monastery is a vibrant center for Buddhist studies and meditation, dedicated to preserving ancient rituals, teachings, and the rich cultural heritage of its tradition. Its peaceful ambiance offers a perfect retreat for spiritual seekers and tourists alike.

History & Culture

The monastery was built to serve as a principal seat for the followers of the Zurmang Kagyu tradition in Sikkim. It stands as a testament to the flourishing of Tibetan Buddhism in the region. The monastery is not just a place of worship but also a thriving educational institution where monks receive comprehensive training in Buddhist philosophy, rituals, and arts.

Architectural Features

Spread over a large area, Lingdum Monastery is a spectacular example of modern Tibetan Buddhist architecture. It is characterized by its intricate murals, vibrant thangkas (Tibetan scroll paintings), and grand statues of various Buddhist deities. The main prayer hall is particularly impressive, filled with detailed artwork that creates a deeply spiritual and serene atmosphere for prayer and meditation.
