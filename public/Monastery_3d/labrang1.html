Labrang Monastery

About Labrang Monastery

Labrang Monastery, located in the scenic region of North Sikkim, is a revered site belonging to the Nyingma sect of Tibetan Buddhism. Established in the 19th century, this historic monastery stands as a significant spiritual and cultural center, attracting pilgrims and travelers seeking peace and insight into Buddhist traditions. Its name translates to "the lama's dwelling."

History & Culture

Founded around 1814 by Gyalshe Rigzing Chempa, Labrang Monastery is one of the premier monastic institutions of the Nyingma school in Sikkim. It is a vital center for the Nyingma tradition, dedicated to preserving ancient rituals, sacred texts, and profound teachings. The monastery is particularly known for its collection of well-preserved murals and serves as an important pilgrimage destination.

Architectural Features

The monastery showcases classic Tibetan architectural styles, characterized by intricate woodwork and vibrant, detailed murals that adorn its walls. These artworks depict various Buddhist deities, mandalas, and spiritual teachings, creating a visually stunning and deeply contemplative atmosphere within the prayer halls and corridors.
