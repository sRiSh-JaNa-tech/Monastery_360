Rumtek Monastery

About Rumtek Monastery

Rumtek Monastery, also known as the Dharmachakra Centre, is a vibrant and sprawling Tibetan Buddhist monastery located near Gangtok, Sikkim. It is the seat-in-exile of the Gyalwa Karmapa, the head of the Karma Kagyu lineage of Tibetan Buddhism. The monastery is a breathtaking replica of the original Tsurphu Monastery in Tibet and serves as one of the most important centers of Kagyu learning and practice outside of Tibet.

History & Culture

The original monastery was founded in the mid-18th century, but the present structure was built in the 1960s by the 16th Karmapa, Rangjung Rigpe Dorje, after he fled Tibet. Rumtek is renowned for its traditional monastic education, intricate rituals, and the preservation of rare Buddhist manuscripts and religious artifacts. It also houses the Golden Stupa, which contains the sacred relics of the 16th Karmapa.

Architectural Features

The monastery is a magnificent example of traditional Tibetan architecture. The main temple is a four-storied structure adorned with vibrant murals, thangkas, and silk banners. The complex also includes a monastic college (Shedra), a retreat center, a nunnery, and various administrative buildings. The large courtyard is the venue for important ceremonies and the famous Chaam dances performed by the monks during festivals.
