Pemayangtse Monastery

About Pemayangtse

Pemayangtse Monastery, located in Pelling, Sikkim, is one of the oldest and most significant monasteries of the Nyingma sect of Tibetan Buddhism. Founded in the 17th century, it was originally built for "ta-tshang" lamas, meaning pure monks, and continues to be a sacred center of spiritual practice.

The monastery is renowned for its unique architecture, ancient manuscripts, exquisite sculptures, and a remarkable wooden structure depicting the heavenly abode of Guru Rinpoche. It plays a vital role in Sikkimese culture and is an important site for festivals such as Cham Dance, which reflects deep-rooted Buddhist traditions and rituals.

History

The monastery was first founded as a small shrine, or Lhakhang, by Lama Lhatsun Chempo in the mid-17th century (c. 1650-1651). Lama Lhatsun Chempo was one of the three lamas credited with consecrating the first Chogyal (king) of Sikkim.

Culture

Nyingma Order: Pemayangtse Monastery belongs to the Nyingma Order of Tibetan Buddhism, which is the oldest school of Tibetan Buddhism. It serves as the controlling monastery for all other Nyingma monasteries in Sikkim. "Pure Monks": The monastery was specifically built for "ta-tshang" or "pure monks," who were chosen from the Bhutia community of Sikkim and were required to be celibate and without any physical abnormalities. This practice is still maintained today, and only the monks of Pemayangtse Monastery are entitled to this title. Royal Anointing: For a long time, the head lama of Pemayangtse Monastery held the unique privilege of anointing the Chogyals (kings) of Sikkim with holy water during their coronation.

Architectural Features

The three-storied monastery is a beautiful example of Tibetan Buddhist architecture. It features intricate woodwork, vibrant murals, and statues of saints and Rinpoches. A notable attraction is a seven-tiered wooden structure on the top floor, which depicts the heavenly palace of Guru Rinpoche (Padmasambhava) and took five years to build by a single lama, Dungzin Rimpoche.
