Phodong Monastery

About Phodong Monastery

Phodong Monastery is a significant Buddhist monastery in Sikkim, located approximately 28 kilometres from Gangtok. It belongs to the Kagyupa sect of Tibetan Buddhism and is renowned for its beautiful murals, frescoes, and ancient religious artifacts. The monastery was founded in the early 18th century by the Chogyal Gyurmed Namgyal.

History & Culture

The original monastery was built in the early 18th century, but the structure has been rebuilt and restored over the years. Phodong Monastery holds great religious importance and is a major center for the Kagyupa sect in Sikkim. The annual festival, held on the 28th and 29th day of the tenth month of the Tibetan calendar, features vibrant Cham dances and attracts devotees from all over the region.

Architectural Features

The monastery is an impressive example of Tibetan architecture, with its brightly colored exterior, intricate woodwork, and stunning murals that adorn the interior walls. These artworks depict scenes from Buddhist mythology and the lives of various saints and deities, creating a spiritually charged atmosphere for visitors and monks alike.
